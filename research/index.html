Power Operations

  • The original idea comes from Steenrod: Products of Cocycles and Extensions of Mappings . He define cup-i products of regular-i pairs on ordered simplicial complex and then the square of cup-i product gives Steenrod squares.

  • N.Steenrod: Reduced Powers of Cohomology Classes. Steenrod introduced the notion of operator complexes in this article. In this way, the category of chain complexes is enriched by itself. Moreover, Steenrod introduced a way to represent a cohomology class.

  • N.Steenrod: Cohomology Operations Derived from the Symmetric Group. In this article, we know that all the cohomology operations are generated by reduced power operations, Bockstein-Whitney operations and coefficient homomorphisms (Main theorem in section 4). A systematic way to construct cohomology operations is in Epstein-Steenrod's Cohomology Operations.

  • N.Steenrod: Cohomology operations, and obstructions to extending continuous functions. This is a good survey on cohomology operations.

  • Peter May: A General Algebraic Approach to Steenrod Operations. This article implicitly says that every $E_\infty$-algebra over $\mathbb{F}_p$ has Steenrod operations. The Steenrod operations are derived from the cohomology on the operad complexes actually.

  • Tayler Lawson: $E_n$ ring spectra and Dyer–Lashof operations.

  • Charles Rezk: Lectures on power operations. This lecture note begin with power operations on equivariant K-theory.

    Michael Mandell: Cochain Multiplications. This article gives axioms of cochain theory and shows that the singular cochain complex is an $E_\infty$-algebra, which is the reason why ordinary cohomology has Steenrod operations (Recall the previous item).

    Michael Mandell: Cochain and Homotopy Type. This article shows that the $E_\infty$-structure of singular cochain complexes determines the homotopy types of nilpotent spaces, which shows why Steenrod operations(as parts of $E_\infty$-structure) are powerful algebraic invariants. [Slides]

    Operad Theory

  • The original idea: Homtopy Everything H-Spaces ,Boardman-Vogt;

  • Peter May's construction of operad (operator + monad): The Geometry of Iterated Loop Spaces. The key points in this paper: littile n-cubes operads, two-side bar constructions and recognization principle (the equivalence between $E_n$-spaces and n-loop spaces). In particular, this shows that the notion of May spectrum, the notion of $E_\infty$-space and the notion of infinite loop spaces are equivalent.

  • Igor Kriz-Peter May: Operads, Algebras, Modules, and Motives ;

  • Michael Mandell's survey: Operads and Operadic Algebras in Homotopy Theory ;

  • Bruno Vallette's survey: Algebra + Homotopy = Operad . There is something related to mathematical physics, for example, homotopy transfer theorem in the context of Feynman diagrams and BV formulism.

  • Barratt-Eccles: $\Gamma^+$-Structures—I: A free group functor for stable homotopy theory . It gives Barratt-Eccles operad $\{E\Sigma_n\}_{n\geq 0}$, and the corresponding monad $\Gamma^+(X)=\coprod E\Sigma_n\times X^n/\sim$ is equivalent to the monad $\Omega^\infty\Sigma^\infty$ (the corresponding operad is little cubes operad).

  • McClure-Smith: Multivariable cochain operations and little $n$-cubes. It provides an explicit way to perform cup-$i$ products and a proof to Deligne's conjecture for Hochschild complexes proposed by Konstsevich in Operads and Motives in Deformation.

  • Charles Rezk's thesis: Spaces of algebra structures and cohomology of operads. Given an operad $A$, the thesis compares two notion of spaces of algebra structures over $A$. One is the classification spaces, the other is moduli spaces. Moreover, the thesis provides a way to compute Quillen cohomology via Hochschild cohomology.

    • Stable Homotopy Theory

  • Peter May: Stable Algebraic Topology. Good historical review of stable homotopy theory.

  • J.P.C Greenlees: Spectra for Communicative Algebraists. This survey introduces three ways to construct the categogry of spectra: EKMM spectra, symmetric spectra and orthogonal spectra. The main difference among them is how to construct the smash products. The central idea is that ring spectra are topological derivation of rings.

  • A.D.Elmendorf, I. Kriz, M. A. Mandell, J. P. May (EKMM): Mordern Foundations for Stable Homotopy Theory. A short survey on the theory of EKMM spectra (about 40 pages).

  • A.D.Elmendorf, I. Kriz, M. A. Mandell, J. P. May (EKMM): Rings, Modules and Algebras in Stable Homotopy Theory. A standard textbook on EKMM spectra.

  • Lewis Jr L.G.: Is there a convenient category of spectra. This article shows that there is NO a symmetric monoidal category with respect to the smash product satisfying some axioms, for example, $\Sigma^\infty$ and $\Omega^\infty$ is a pair of adjunctions; $\Sigma^\infty S^0$ should be the unit for the smash product; some existence of natural equivalences and weak equivalences, etc.

  • Jacob Lurie: Derived Algebraic Geometry I: Stable $\infty$-Categories. Although there is no a convenient category of spectra, there is a convenient $\infty$-category of spectra. That's a reason why we need the framework of higher algebra.

  • N.P.Strickland: An Introduction to the Category of Spectra. There a lots of examples of spectra in algebraic topology. To invert the suspension functor $\Sigma\!:\mathbb{S}pace\rightarrow \mathbb{S}pace$ i.e. make $\Sigma$ be an equivalence between categories, stable homotopy category. Namely, in stable homotopy category, $\Sigma X$ is homotopy equivalent to $X$. If we view $\Sigma$ as $-\wedge S^1$, we may say that we get the stable homotopy category by inverting spheres.

  • Mark Hovey: Spectra and symmetric spectra in general model categories. This article gives two general ways for the passage from unstable homotopy category to stable homotopy category. It does not only work on topological spaces, but also on motivic spaces and $\mathbb{A}^1$ homotopy theory.

  • Daniel Dugger: Stable categories and spectra via model categories. The viewpoint from model categories.

    • Motivic World

  • J.Milnor: Algebraic K-theory and Quadratic Forms. This article proposed Milnor conjecture, which is the motivation to construct motivic cohomology and motivic homotopy theory.

  • Voevodsky-Morel: $\mathbb{A}^1$-homotopy Theory of Schemes. The original paper of motivic homotopy theory.

  • M.Levine: Vladimir Voevodsky—An Appreciation. It gives a brief r´esum´e of some of the works of the late Vladimir Voevodsky. 1. Trianglated categories of motives; 2. The slice tower in the motivic stable homotopy category: Atiyah-Hirzebruch spectral sequences, alegraic cobordism, and the Steenrod algebra; 3. Cross functors and Grothendieck's six-functor formalism; 4. Framed correspondences and recognition principles.

  • V.Voevodsky: Voevodsky's Seattle Lectures. This lecture note gives a sketch proof of Milnor conjecture by using motivic cohomology theory.

  • V.Voevodsky: Motivic Cohomology with $\mathbb{Z}/2$-Coefficient. This article revisted the proof of Milnor conjecture.

  • V.Voevodsky: Reduced Power Operations in Motivic Cohomology

  • F.Morel: An Introduction to $\mathbb{A}^1$-Homotopy Theory. It gives some discussion on what the algebraic analogues of spheres are. The possible options are $\mathbb{P}^1$ and $\mathbb{G}_m$.

  • Pauli-Wickelgren: Applications to $\mathbb{A}^1$-enumerative geometry of the $\mathbb{A}^1$-degree. These are lecture notes from the conference Arithmetic Topology at the PIMS on applications of Morel's A1-degree to questions in enumerative geometry. Additionally, we give a new dynamic interpretation of the $\mathbb{A}^1$-Milnor number inspired by the first named author's enrichment of dynamic intersection numbers. Note that the history of the use of Brouwer degree in enumerative geometry in classical topology can be found in Dieudonne's book A History of Algebraic and Differential Topology, 1900-1960 Page 197. This arcticle try to construct motivic version of degree theory in enumerative geometry in algebraic geometry.
